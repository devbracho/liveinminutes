Google's **Veo 3.1** is currently the strongest general text-to-video model: it generates video *and* native audio (dialogue, sound effects, ambient noise) together, with strong physics and prompt adherence. This is the fastest path from idea to a watchable clip.

## 1. Draft the prompt with a chat model

Video models reward specific, structured prompts. Use ChatGPT (or Gemini / Claude) to expand a one-line idea into a shot list. A reliable prompt template:

```text
You are a cinematographer. Turn my idea into a Veo 3.1 prompt.
Idea: <your idea>
Return: subject, action, setting, camera movement, lighting,
lens/style, mood, and any dialogue or sound effects.
Keep it to one vivid paragraph.
```

A good Veo prompt names six things: **subject, action, setting, camera move, lighting, and style.** Add audio in plain language ("she whispers: 'we're not alone'", "rain on a tin roof").

## 2. Generate the clip in Veo 3.1

Veo 3.1 is available in several surfaces. Pick by goal:

| Surface | Best for | Link |
| ------- | -------- | ---- |
| **Google Flow** | Full filmmaking: scenes, camera controls, extend clips | [labs.google/flow](https://labs.google/flow) |
| **Gemini app** | Quick single clips | [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com) |
| **Google AI Studio / Gemini API** | Automating generation from code | [ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/video](https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/video) |

Veo 3.1 adds controls worth using:

- **Ingredients to video**: feed reference images of a character, object, or scene to keep them consistent.
- **First and last frame**: provide two images and Veo animates the transition between them.
- **Extend**: continue a clip past its original length while keeping visual and audio continuity.
- **1080p / 4K** output for editing-grade footage.

## 3. Keep characters and style consistent

For anything longer than one shot, lock identity first:

1. Generate or pick a single clean reference image of your character.
2. Use it as an "ingredient" in every shot so the face and outfit stay stable.
3. Pass a **style reference image** (a frame whose look you like) so lighting and grade match across cuts.

## 4. Other models worth trying

Veo is not the only option, and the leaders shift monthly. Compare before committing:

- **Runway Gen-4** ([runwayml.com](https://runwayml.com)) for strong stylized motion and editing tools.
- **Kling 2.x** ([klingai.com](https://klingai.com)) for long, smooth motion at lower cost.
- **OpenAI Sora** ([sora.com](https://sora.com)) for storyboarded scenes inside ChatGPT.
- **Luma Dream Machine** ([lumalabs.ai](https://lumalabs.ai)) for fast iteration.

## 5. Assemble and ship

Generate clips of 5 to 8 seconds, then stitch them:

- **CapCut** or **DaVinci Resolve** (free) for cutting and color.
- **OpusClip** for turning a long video into short vertical cuts.

Render once, upload to YouTube/TikTok, done. Note: Veo outputs carry an invisible **SynthID** watermark marking them as AI-generated.

> Verify model names and limits before a paid run; video models update frequently and per-clip pricing changes.
